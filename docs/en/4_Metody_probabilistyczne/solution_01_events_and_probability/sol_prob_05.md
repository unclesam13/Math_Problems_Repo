# Solution 5 – Operations on Probabilities

## Given

$$
P(A) = 0.6
$$

$$
P(B) = 0.7
$$

$$
P(A \cup B) = 0.8
$$



## 1. Complement of A

The complement formula:

$$
P(A') = 1 - P(A)
$$

Therefore:

$$
P(A') = 1 - 0.6 = 0.4
$$



## 2. Intersection $P(A \cap B)$

Using the union formula:

$$
P(A \cup B) =
P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)
$$

Substitute values:

$$
0.8 = 0.6 + 0.7 - P(A \cap B)
$$

Thus:

$$
P(A \cap B) = 0.5
$$



## 3. $P(A' \cap B')$

Using De Morgan's law:

$$
A' \cap B' = (A \cup B)'
$$

Therefore:

$$
P(A' \cap B') =
1 - P(A \cup B)
$$

$$
P(A' \cap B') = 1 - 0.8
$$

$$
P(A' \cap B') = 0.2
$$

## Final Result

$$
P(A') = 0.4
$$

$$
P(A \cap B) = 0.5
$$

$$
P(A' \cap B') = 0.2
$$
