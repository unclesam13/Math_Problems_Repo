Pick two statements and inspect the operation step by step.
Step 1. Mark the first statement.
Step 2. Add the second statement and inspect overlap.
Step 3. Keep exactly the cells demanded by the set operation.
Union keeps a cell if at least one statement allows or selects it.
Intersection keeps a cell only if both statements keep it.
Difference keeps cells from the first statement after removing those also present in the second.
The conceptual shift here is important: we are no longer listing whole outcomes directly. We are organizing a family of day-by-day constraints, and the table makes those constraints visible.
