List 4 · Problem 4

From Simple Statements to Complex Ones

This problem is where logical language and set language really meet. We start with three basic events: A for “sum is 7”, B for “first die is greater than the second”, and C for “at least one die shows 6”. Then we build more complex statements by using union, intersection, complement, and negation.

How to read this page

Go from top to bottom: first inspect the basic events A, B, C, then choose a compound statement, and finally walk through its construction step by step with Previous Step and Next Step.
